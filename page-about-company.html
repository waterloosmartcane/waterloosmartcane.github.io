Hi, We're Smart Cane

We're building a portable mechatronics feedback system to help visually-impaired individuals.

Built on passion and ingenuity

In 2019, 1.2 million Canadians had some form of visual loss, while 4.1% of the group is blind. The visually impaired are also 50% less likely to take independent trips and 70% more likely to fall as the current white cane does not support them as they traverse areas with unknown or difficult obstacles and terrains and general wayfinding indoors and outdoors. Traditional white canes' object detection capabilities are limited to the physical range of the cane. This can lead to falls and other injuries that severely hinders their quality of life.

What drives us

This system accounts for portability, providing audio and haptic feedback, proactively preventing falls, being reliable against misplacement, detecting obstacles, and being comfortable during use.

Inclusion

The visually-impaired should be able to go on longer walks without fear of falling.

Ingenuity

Using our Mechatronics Engineering background, we understood how to use deep learning, sensor fusion, and mechanical and electrical hardware to build a prototype.

Innovation

Our work builds on market searches and previous state-of-the-art patents. We enable novel short-range and long-range obstacle avoidance.
