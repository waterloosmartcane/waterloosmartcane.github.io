Smart Cane:
Object Detection for Visually-Impaired

How are we making a social impact?

Mechatronics Principles Applied To Old Devices

The visually-impaired are 70% more likely to fall and 50% less likely to take independent trips. The current white cane does not offer adequate support to avoid obstacles. Traditional white canes can only provide information in a local area where the cane is pointing, limiting the ability to detect poor ground conditions in advance. This limitation can lead to injuries and subsequently affect their quality of life. Smart Cane will use robotic sensors and hardware to provide real-time feedback.

Robotics Sensors

A self-navigating smart cane that fuses LiDAR, stereo cameras, and IMU sensor data using Kalman filters.

Advanced Software

Road sign detection by implementing the pre-trained deep learning model, YoloFastestV2 for short-term and long-term distances

State-of-the-Art Hardware

Jetson Nano for obstacle detection, raspberry pi for sensor fusion, powerbank battery, motors for vibration and steering cane. There will also be an omnidirectional wheel for ease of travel.
